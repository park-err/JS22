Factors Affecting Reaction Rates

Introduction

What factors affect the reate of a reaction? In this laboratory experiment, I studied the effect of concentration, temperature, and a catalyst on the reaction rate.

Materials

  • Potassium iodate solution, 0.05 M KIO3, 28 mL
  • Sodium meta-bisulfate solution, 0.05 M Na2S2O5, 10 mL
  • Ice
  • Starch solution, 10 mL
  • Sulfuric Acid, 0.1 M H2SO4, 1 mL
  • 5 100 mL beakers
  • 1 250 mL beaker
  • 2 10 mL graduated cylinders
  • 2 25 mL graduated cylinders
  • Hot plate
  • Microplate
  • White paper
  • Stirring rod
  • 2 syringes
  • Thermometer

Procedure

Concentration

  1. Mix together 15 mL of sodium meta-bisulfate and 15 mL of starch solution
  2. Prepare microplate: put 5 mL KIO3 in well 1, 4 mL KIO3 in well 2, 3 mL KIO3 in well 3, 2 mL KIO3 in well 4, and 1 mL KIO3 in well 5.
  3. In the microplate, use deionized water to fill the all of the wells to 5 mL. Well 1 should have no water, well 2 should have 1 mL, etc. Record temperature for later section.
  4. Stir each well with a stirring rod.
  5. Add 2 mL of sodium meta-bisulfate/starch solution mixture to each well, and time each reaction.

Temperature

  1. Empty the microplate
  2. Prepare a hot bath and cold bath
  3. Put 5 mL KIO3 in a hot bath and 5 mL KIO3 in a cold bath
  4. Put the KIO3 in the well plates and record the temperatures
  5. Add 2 mL of meta-bisulfate/starch solution and record reaction times

Catalyst

  1. Empty the microplate
  2. Prepare two wells with 1 mL of KIO3 each
  3. Add 4 mL of deionized water to well 1, and 3 mL of deioinized water to well 2. Stir.
  4. Add 1 mL of H2SO4 to the well 2.
  5. Add 2 mL of meta-bisulfate/starch solution and record reaction times

Conclusion

The concentration and the temperature of the reactants have a linear relationship with the reaction rate. Furthermore, adding a catalyst to the reaction causes the reaction rate to increase as well.
